Pizza

Pizza (Italian: ['pittsa], Neapolitan: ['pittse]) is a savory dish of Italian origin consisting of a usually round, flattened base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and often various other ingredients (such as anchovies, mushrooms, onions, olives, pineapple, meat, etc.), which is then baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven. [1] A small pizza is sometimes called a pizzetta. A person who makes pizza is known as a pizzaiolo.

Pizza

Pizza is a popular Italian dish consisting of a leavened, flattened disk of bread dough topped with sauce, cheese, and various other ingredients, then baked at a high temperature.Originating in Naples, Italy, as a humble dish for the poor in the 18th century, it evolved from ancient flatbread traditions and became a worldwide favorite after American soldiers in World War II discovered it in Italy. A classic example is the Margherita, topped with tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil, inspired by Queen Margherita of Savoy.
